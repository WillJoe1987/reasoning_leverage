The problem involves a 19×19 board where each square contains a black or white stone. An operation consists of selecting one row and one column, then flipping the color of all 37 stones in that row and column (the intersection stone is flipped only once). The goal is to find the largest integer n such that, regardless of the initial configuration, it is always possible to perform a sequence of operations to ensure at least n black stones.

2. **Parity Analysis**: Each stone's color is flipped based on the number of times its row and column are chosen. Let r_i (1 ≤ i ≤ 19) be the number of times row i is chosen, and c_j (1 ≤ j ≤ 19) be the number of times column j is chosen. The final color of stone (i,j) is flipped r_i + c_j times (mod 2).
3. **Linear Algebra Approach**: Represent the problem over GF(2). The state of each stone is a linear equation: initial_color + r_i + c_j ≡ final_color (mod 2). We aim to maximize the number of black stones (final_color = 1).
4. **Dimension of Solution Space**: There are 19 + 19 = 38 variables (r_i and c_j), but the system has a dependency: sum(r_i) + sum(c_j) ≡ total_flips (mod 2). The solution space has dimension 38 - 1 = 37.
